Die Ratten

90 min.

The Rats ("Die Ratten") is Gerhart Hauptmann's most-performed play. In the tragic comedy, life themes collide and are still relevant more than a hundred years after the first performance. It's about unwanted pregnancy and an unfulfilled desire to have children, about illicit love and unheard-of longing, about care and emancipation, about being a parent - and again and again about the theatre, about perspectives on art, life and the world.

In this extraordinary theater, the social participation of people with disabilities is lived on a daily basis and finds its unique, artistic expression in the approximately 100 performances per season. The theater is one of the nationally and internationally most renowned stages for inclusive theatre. The heart of the theater are the more than 30 actors who, with their humour,their energy and their very special passion, immensely enrich every classic piece and every contemporary text and create new sparks from it. The continuous cooperation with young, innovative and courageous directors, choreographers, musicians and authors makes the theater a place for modern, non-conformist and challenging productions.
