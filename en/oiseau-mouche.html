Description of the theatre:

La Compagnie de l’Oiseau-Mouche is a company created in 1978 with an ensemble of 23 learning-disabled professional performers.

The high level of commitment of our actors and the quality of their work have created a path for them to access mainstream venues that had never been opened to learning-disabled performers before.

The project remains unique in France. The company has no permanent artistic director and each creation is the result of a unique encounter with an invited artist.

L’Oiseau-Mouche has created 50 shows, performed over 1,700 times and is supported by the French Ministry of Culture.

Brief description of the play:

A dot inside a circle. You are here. Or maybe there. It all depends on where we are.

A line on the floor delineates spaces with unstable borders and visible edges, trajectories to invent and territories to roam and conquer.

An object of power and control as well as the representation of a reality or the reflection of an imagined notion, the map is always changing, and is used to orient oneself, navigate, commerce, wage war, or even escape and dream.

Bouger les lignes leads four guides, themselves leading adults and children alike, into the exploration of those geostrategic or poetic itineraries. They decipher captions, change scales, and multiply perspectives.

For the wish of director Bérangère Vantusso is to lead us into moving zones, both physically and emotionally, with images by artists Paul Cox and words by writer Nicolas Doutey.

Together, and with the Compagnie de l’Oiseau-Mouche, the idea is to reshuffle the cards to rethink the world.

Malika Baziz for the Festival d’Avignon
