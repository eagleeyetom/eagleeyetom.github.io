Documentary "Two days a week"

20 min.

The documentary film “Two Days a Week”, presented online at Masska, is a summary of the artistic work of the Poor Relations Theatre during its residency at the Atelier Theatre and was created to celebrate the 30th Anniversary of the Theatre's activity. The film was entirely created by Gabriela Kucz.

history

The Poor Relations Theatre is a spontaneous, annually renewed project that has been running uninterrupted for 30 years. Its aim is to express the feelings, thoughts, and experiences of its actors. It is the result of searching for ways to talk about what is important in life and to present beliefs that cannot be expressed otherwise.

The theatre is one of the oldest of its kind in Poland and in the world. It has participated in many festivals both domestically and internationally: Łódź, Lublin, Warsaw, Przemyśl, Lidzbark Warmiński, Ostróda, Gdynia, Gdańsk, Horyniec Zdrój, Stalowa Wola, etc., as well as performances in Lithuania and Finland.

The oldest actor has been performing for 30 years, while the youngest for 4 months. The theatre operates at the Type B Community Self-Help Center in Sopot, PZW PKPS. Since January 2024, it has been an artistic resident at the Atelier Theatre in Sopot. It is also the organizer of the INTERNATIONAL THEATRE REVIEW MASSKA IN SOPOT.
