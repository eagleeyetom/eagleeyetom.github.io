Founded:

Created in 1982 as a youth dance band. Since 1991, it has been working at a professional level. Since 2003, it has been working in an integrated team.

Theatre's personnel:

Professional team - 10 people. Leader and director, seven professional dancers, one of them is a composer, singer, musician and leader of our small inclusive orchestra. Three dancers help as teachers. Also a professional theatre artist and vocal teacher cooperates with us.

26 children with various forms of physical and mental disabilities are engaged in the theatre. Five of them have reached a professional level. The founder and director of the theatre is Lilia Sevastyanova.

For the last two years, the Lik Movement Theatre has been operating under the Tashkent and Uzbekistan Diocese of the Russian Orthodox Church.

Description of the theatre's activity:

For 19 years, the theatre has been working in an integrated composition, uniting professional dancers, actors and young people with different forms of disabilities. Educational and staging work is based on the author's technique of dance improvisation, which is a common language for very different people. The theatre has a large repertoire, all performances show the value and uniqueness of the soul of every person, regardless of his/her social status and state of health.

The theatre is the winner and participant of many international festivals and socio-cultural projects in Uzbekistan, Russia, Ukraine, Belarus, France and other countries. The activity of the theatre affirms the idea that we can all make up for each other's shortcomings with our virtues, and, thus, come closer to true unity.

Brief description of the play:

The spectacle talks about how young disabled people, entering adulthood, look for a common language with other people and the world at all using the language and music language.

All children with special needs have a dream - revive their talents. During performance they are fulfilling their dreams with help of professional actors
