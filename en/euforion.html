EUFORION theatre's wanderings – Ciaptak in Stara Kamienica

14 min.

"Ciaptak" by Jan Brzechwa is a spectacle in which we think about "otherness" and social exclusion.

Many of us want to be someone unusual and unique. Sometimes "being different" brings admiration and awe, at other times it becomes a reason for social rejection. We are afraid of otherness, we miss it. Sometimes it brings us joy, sometimes brings loneliness.

The recording was created in the gymnasium's gym in Stara Kamienica, where students organised the project "Disabled in the world". The project was aimed at showing young people the lives of people with physically and mentally disabled, as well as teaching sensitivity and empathy to another person.

  • Anna Jędryczka-Hamera - clinical psychologist, artistic manager: director, screenwriter, stage designer, costume designer
  • Beata Radzik - music therapy, organizational manager

The "Euforion" Theatre is a group working for 27 years. The performances include people diagnosed with schizophrenia or bipolar disorder, therapists and students of Wrocław's universities.

In the repertoire the theatre has its own adaptation of world literature, among others "DJ Faust" based on "Faust" by J. W. Goethe, "Don Quixote" based on the text of De Cervantes, "Colais Bregnon" by R. Rolland and "Don Juan" by Molier, telling about people whom we would now call the mentally ill.

The second current of the "Euforion" is the popularization of the work of Polish-speaking authors, whose work speak about social exclusion, a phenomenon that affects the disabled and the mentally ill. The actors of the "crazy" Integration Theatre Euforion prepared and performed in Poland and abroad, e.g. "Rozmowa z kamieniem" by Wisława Szymborska, "Ciaptak" by Jan Brzechwa, "Wilki" by Danuta Wawiłow, "Garby" by Leszek Kołakowski.
